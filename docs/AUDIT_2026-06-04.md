# Morphium Kernel — Verification Round 2 (2026-06-04)

Adversarial audit of the **system-level / device** layer added after the
2026-06-03 materials audit: the thermal-budget co-integration model
(`integration.py`), the device-envelope model (`devices.py`), and their docs —
checked against device physics and literature by 7 independent auditors, with a
separate skeptic re-verifying every flagged finding.
**54 claims checked → 27 solid, 18 overstated, 7 wrong, 2 unverifiable.
Skeptic upheld 17 problems, overturned 4 over-eager criticisms.**

## Bottom line
The *logic* of the new tools is sound (the descending-thermal-budget rule, the
"2 of 120 build orders", the slab-vs-cube thermal reconciliation, "build the watch
first" all survived). The problems were **optimistic numbers stated too firmly** —
two of them by orders of magnitude:

- **Memory density** was the 1F² lithographic *ceiling* (1 Tbit/cm²) presented as an
  achievable cell density → device capacities were ~100× high.
- **IGZO logic clock** was an aggressive projection (0.3 GHz) presented as
  manufacturable → the Si gap was understated as 13× when it is ~400× measured.
- **Plane counts** (4/8/16) multiplied capacity without being reconciled against the
  thermal budget (which supports ~2–4 planes).
- The **optical-MAC matrix side** mis-mapped a propagation limit to a compute dimension.

All 17 confirmed findings are resolved (corrected numbers + honest dual
demonstrated/ceiling reporting + provenance reconciliation). The 4 overturned
criticisms (3 fJ/op floor, Δn 0.747 anchor, 539 mN force, the ops/s derivation) were
shown to be defensible and were **left as-is** — severity was not inflated.

## What did NOT change
The 2026-06-03 materials physics and methodology (audited separately) stand. The
thermal-budget *logic*, the "2 of 120 viable build orders", PM pinned to the cold
top, and the "build the watch first" conclusion all survived this round — only the
quantitative envelopes around them were corrected and re-tagged for honesty.
